Quick Chips:

Photosynthesis

/ˌfoʊtoʊˈsɪnθəsɪs/ • Noun

Definition

The process by which green plants and some other organisms use sunlight to synthesize foods from carbon dioxide and water.

Twi Interpretation

Sɛnea afifide a ɛyɛ ahabammono de awia hann yɛ wɔn aduan.

Literally: "How green plants use sun light to make their food."

Example Sentence

"Without photosynthesis, the atmosphere would not have enough oxygen for humans to breathe."

Academic Context & Insights

In your upcoming Biology Mid-sem (Unit 4), this term is crucial for understanding the Carbon Cycle. Remember that this process occurs primarily in the chloroplasts of leaf cells.

Similar Academic Terms

Chlorophyll

The green pigment responsible for the absorption of light to provide energy for photosynthesis.

Stomata

Microscopic pores found on the under-surface of leaves used for gas exchange.

Respiration

The opposite of photosynthesis; the process where cells break down glucose to release energy.
